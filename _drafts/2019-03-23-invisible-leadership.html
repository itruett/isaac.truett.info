Leadership is Infrastructure for Organizations

Good infrastructure is invisible. You don't think about power lines or sewer lines most days unless there's a problem. You don't say "gosh, the power company did a really good job keeping the lights on yesterday." A good leader in an organization is like that. You know who's in charge, but when things are going well and the leaders are doing their jobs correctly, it's not readily apparent. This is not to say that they are — or appear to be — doing nothing. Instead, the leader's most effective actions may be behind the scenes, less obvious to the people further away in the organization.

The Old Ways Are No Longer Enough

More important to know the right questions to ask, not just to have all of the answers.

If good leadership doesn't catch attention, how do you recognize it? How do you find the role models that serve as examples for good leadership?

Look for people who help others find answers instead of always giving them.
